😯 How I discovered the book ?

A friend recommended it to me once when we were on a bus on our way home.

🤔 General thoughts

The story is very well written and enormously touching, I could live with lily and feel what she felt, cry when she cried and smile when she smiled. It discussed several social topics that i never paid attention to.

👬 To whom I would recommend it to ?

To anyone who want to know some of the reasons that may push a women into baring conjugal violence and have compassion with the homeless knowing it’s not totally their fault and that the situation they live in is out of their hands.

📕 Summary And Notes: (spoilers)

lily who grew in a house with an abusive father, and mother week in her eyes for not leaving him. As lily grew older she fell in love with a neurosurgeon she met the night of her father’s funeral in sealing and 6 month after that when she decided to open her floral shop with Ryle’s sister as an employee. But soon enough she discovers his anger issues the night he slapped her face when she made fun of his hand injury while cooking dinner before an important operation. Convincing herself it was a mistake that will never be repeated, she forgave him. Business is doing great and Ryle wants to have dinner with her mother at restaurant whose chef is lily’s ex Atlas, a homeless boy she saved when he was 18 and she was 15 providing him with food, shower and company watching The Ellen show. Their love never faded feeding Ryle’s Jealousy ending with beating then raping that lily bared finding reasons and justifications to stay in the house of her love life but ended up forcing lily to run out to her ex’s house then finding out she was pregnant. when she had her baby, she finally had her mind made, she asked for divorce at the first time Ryle took his daughter in his hands as it’s the best solution for their child’s future. Finally she got back with atlas and lived happily ever after.

💬 Best 3 Quotes:

> Everything is almost better in Boston. Except the girls. Boston doesn’t have you. > There is no such thing as bad people. We’re all just people who sometimes do bad things. > The last thing you want to do is to lose sight of your limit. Please don’t allow that to happen.

Lessons I’ve learned from this book

It’s not always as easy as “I’m hurt I would leave” in any situation either homelessness or romantic. We solely see and judge based on our outer perspective ignoring the full image. We should have sympathy for others, never brag or accuse. One should be resilient and improve his life no matter how bad his previous experiences were. We should never loose sight of our limits in a relationship and know when to quite no matter the consequences. Never blame yourself for other’s mistakes
